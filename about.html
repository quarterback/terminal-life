What is Against Interface?

Against Interface is a slow zine for fast systems.

We write about governance, software, bureaucracy, infrastructure, and the quiet violence of interfaces. Our world is increasingly shaped by flows we didn’t choose, and friction we’re told to avoid.

This project documents what’s left of the analog world, and what interfaces replace it.

It is published sometimes. On purpose.

Founded and edited by Tristen B. Vornanen. All inquiries are discouraged.
