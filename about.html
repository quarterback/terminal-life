About

Updated April 2025

Out of Scope is a critical zine about the politics of infrastructure, digital friction, and the aestheticization of resistance. It’s a slow-burn response to the fast-churn world of platform logic, designed to interrogate what we’ve lost in the pursuit of seamlessness.

This project lives between design criticism, systems analysis, and speculative documentation. The essays here often trace quiet patterns beneath loud interfaces — platform failure, pseudo-participation, data illusions, institutional decay, and techno-political sleight of hand.

The interface didn’t glitch. It obeyed power.

Founded in 2025, Out of Scope is edited, designed, and written by Tristen B. Vornanen, a pseudonymous researcher working across interface governance, service design, and strategic foresight. The project is affiliated with the fictional Sarkolin Institute.
