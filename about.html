Out of Scope is a critical zine about digital life at the edge of collapse. It collects research fragments, speculative essays, and infrastructural autopsies—written in response to the interfaces, policies, and platforms that shape power in the background of everyday life.

Originally conceived as a companion to the Consequence Design Reader, this project expands into broader terrain: how systems break, how authority is aestheticized, and how infrastructure fails while pretending not to.

The interface didn’t glitch. It obeyed power.

This site is organized around two guides: QX, a structured archive of essays on consequence design, and Theory, a more fragmented cavern of speculative notes, half-built concepts, and ongoing thought experiments.
